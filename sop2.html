Sample Submission for Mass Spectrometry Analysis

Process for Submission

Mass spectrometers have low detection limits; however, the ionization of analytes may be suppressed by contaminants (including common buffer salts and detergents) present in samples.

Many commonly used reagents especially for biological samples have poor compatibility with mass spectrometric ionization techniques. Additionally, the mass spectrometer can be contaminated or clogged if crude samples containing non-volatile components are introduced into the instrument.

  • First, go through the MS facility guidelines or contact the facility manager for sample preparation and protocols.
  • Download the Sample submission form from the ILS website or request it by email to the MS Facility.
  • Complete the Sample submission form, sign it, and pay the applicable charges.
  • If you are submitting more than one sample, enclose a separate list of samples with respective sample details in an Excel sheet format.
  • Give unique names to sample tubes (1.5 ml microfuge tubes preferred).
  • Samples should be prepared strictly as per the protocol compatible with the Mass spectrometer. In case, you are not aware of the correct protocols, use the protocol standardized at the ILS MS Facility.
  • Samples in dry/lyophilised form with proper tube labeling and matching with the enclosed list will be accepted.
  • Once the sample is processed, results obtained will be sent by email to PI upon job completion.
  • The time required to complete the job from the submission of the sample, along with the filled and signed request form, to results mailed to PI.
  • The below-mentioned time for job completion may vary in case of sample queue, system failure, or any other unavoidable circumstances.

Precautions
  • Contact MS facility and discuss regarding sample preparation and protocol.
  • Submit sample convenient time after discussing with MS facility along with signed sample submission form.
  • Concentration 1 mg/ml sample is required.
  • Sample send with 50mm Ammonium bicarbonate having PH-8.
  • Always use LC MS Grade water for sample preparation.
  • Always use glass container and rinsed with 70% ACN or methanol.
  • Avoid keratin contamination by using mask, gloves, caps.
  • Use Only volatile salts (e.g., ammonium bicarbonate, ammonium format, ammonium acetate, triethylammonium bicarbonate (TEAB)) are compatible with mass spectrometers.
  • If your sample contain non-volatile salts use c18 Zip tip, and suitable cut of membrane and buffer exchange.
  • The species data is required for user.
  • The user should provide a protein database (in. fasta format).
  • Use powder free nitrile Gloves, mask, cap, shoe cover for MS sample preparation.
  • After desalting and buffer exchange, do protein quantification.
  • Freshly prepare all the buffers for MS analysis with MS grade reagent.
  • Desalt samples prior to sample submission.
  • The sample to be shipped in refrigerator condition.
  • Sample will not be analysed if payment is not received.
  • Don’t store buffers in plastic container and do not seal either parafin with solvents.
  • Non-volatile salts and additives (e.g., sodium (Na+), potassium (K+), or phosphate) can precipitate at the high‐organic end of the solvent gradient. This will block the columns and MS source.
  • Do not wash Flask and tube with detergent or soap.

    • To Avoid Keratin Contamination

    Keratin is found in almost all samples because humans shed pieces of skin / hair. Common recommendations to reduce contamination include:

  • Wear nitrile gloves (latex may introduce latex proteins).
  • Do not lean over samples, skin particles may fall from your head introducing keratin.
  • Keep solutions in closed containers to exclude dust.
  • Keep lab ware covered or enclosed to exclude dust, and close pipet tip boxes.
  • Work in an area cleaned of dust before hand, preferably a laminar flow hood.
  • Avoid long sleeves and wool when handling samples; long sleeves funnel hairs and skin flakes toward the sample.
  • Use tubes that have been kept sealed from possible dust.
  • When opening a tube containing a sample, blow with clean air to remove dust from the tube.
